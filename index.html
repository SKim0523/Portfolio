Sunny Day

“Sunny Day” app provides parents of small kids a simple reminder tool to record their kids’ daily and weekly schedule as well as any relevant memos for a given day. Users can create, edit, and delete their memos/schedules and toggle between daily view and weekly view.

Technologies: Python| Django | PostgreSQL | CSS | HTML

Art of the Day

"Art of the Day" app provides users an opportunity to learn about a piece of new art work everyday by retrieving a random art from the Art Institute of Chicago API. Users can also search art works by entering a search term inside a search box and click on a specific art to see selected detail information on the art.

Technologies: React| JavaScript | CSS | HTML | Bootstrap

More About Me

I live in North Bethesda, MD with my husband John and my three-year-old son Daniel. John has been working on a government customs and border protection project as a software engineer for the last two years. I thank him for inspiring me to explore the world of software engineering and making me laugh every day with his quirky sense of humor. Daniel is a happy and mischievous little person. He is very enthusiastic about all kinds of sea animals (especially starfish) and plants.

I have 8 years of experience working as a Korean Linguist under my belt. The majority of my work, including research, translation, transcription, analysis, and quality control tasks, has catered to the stringent requirements of Federal Government clients. Through these experiences, I acquired oral and written communication skills that can effectively process complicated information and deliver it in a simpler manner for the clients and stakeholders. Can the 'complicated information' include codes as well? Yes, I believe so!

Human Rights issues, and especially that of North Korea, has always been close to my heart. You may already be aware of what is going on in that small country in North East Asia, but click here if you are curious to learn more on the human rights situation there.

In my years as a graduate student at George Washington University, I had the previlege of assisting David Hawk, a former Executive Director of Amnesty International, USA, and a former official of the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights, in publishing a report on political prison camp systems in North Korea. The report has been published some time ago now, but I wanted to share one of my dearest none-code-related projects here as well.

FACT 1: I have two Taekwondo black belts. I started Takwondo in my early twenties because I wanted to overcome fears and convince myself that I can do anything if I put my mind to it.

FACT 2: I developed several lottery games that generated millions of dollars of revenue for the Washington DC Lottery. Some of them are still in the market contributing to the 'General Fund' of the DC government!

FACT 3: As a child, my dream was to become an entomologist. My father, who've always encouraged my curiosity as I grew up, got a translated edition of "Souvenirs entomologiques" by Jean-Henri Fabre and the book quickly became my childhood favorite.
